Prosper Loan Python Project

As a participant in a bootcamp program, I was given the task to analyze a loan dataset using Python. The dataset was large, containing 81 columns and 113,938 rows, but I was determined to find meaningful insights. To begin my analysis, I imported necessary packages such as Pandas and NumPy. After loading the dataset, I explored its structure and took note of the key features that I believed would help me understand the types of loans borrowers had acquired. These included loan terms, borrower's employment and homeownership status, credit scores, income range, annual percentage rate and interest rate. I then applied data visualization techniques using Matplotlib and Seaborn to help me visualize the data and gain insights. This allowed me to see correlations between different features, such as the relationship between loan status and borrower's credit score. In the end, my analysis was successful in identifying important trends and relationships within the dataset. I was able to provide valuable insights to my team and contribute to our understanding of the loan market. This project helped me to develop my skills in data analysis and visualization and gave me the confidence to tackle future challenges in the field.

COVID SQL Project

COVID-19 struck the world, causing unprecedented disruption to daily life and sparking a global health crisis. After the COVID-19 pandemic had subsided, I became curious about the data related to the event. It was such an important event in our lives that I wanted to utilize my new skills in data analysis to gain insights into the impact of the virus. Using Microsoft SQL Server, I analyzed COVID-19 data from the CovidDeaths and Vaccinations tables. With this project, I created various queries to extract insights on total cases and deaths, population infected, and death rates by country and continent. I used window functions to calculate rolling vaccination counts and percentages of population vaccinated, and also utilized CTEs and temporary tables for more complex queries. In the end, I was able to create a view to store the data for later visualizations. This project allowed me to gain valuable experience in data analysis and showcase my skills in SQL Server while contributing to a better understanding of the impact of COVID-19.

Churn Dashboard in PwC Virtual Internship

During my virtual internship at PwC Switzerland, I had the opportunity to work on a project for a company called PayNow. They were experiencing high customer churn rates and wanted to know why customers were leaving. As someone who is passionate about solving problems, I was excited to dive in and find the root cause of the issue. Using Power Query to clean the data, I created a churn dashboard with Power BI that provided a comprehensive view of customer churn, service subscriptions, account information, and demographic data. With this dashboard, we were able to identify key insights and opportunities for business improvement. Upon analyzing the data, I found some interesting trends. For example, customers on a month-to-month contract were the most at risk of churning. Additionally, churned customers contacted technical support more often than returning customers. Perhaps most alarming was the fact that 42% of senior citizens were churning. I suggested that PayNow look further into the technical support tickets to find the root of the issues. By providing additional support for employees in that area, they could cater to customers more efficiently, especially for senior citizens. I also recommended providing an incentive to stay with PayNow on a yearly contract rather than a month-to-month basis, which could attract and keep customers longer. Overall, this project allowed me to apply my skills in data analysis and visualization to help a company solve a real business problem.

Puppy's Adult Weight Calculator

As a proud new owner of a beagle puppy, I was concerned about his weight and wanted to make sure he would grow up to be a healthy dog. That's why I decided to take on a Python project to create a calculator that could predict his adult weight based on his current weight and age. Using my programming skills, I created a command-line program that utilized a basic formula to calculate the expected adult weight of my puppy. I also incorporated error handling to ensure that the input data was valid and accurate. The program outputted a message categorizing my puppy's expected size based on the calculated weight. This allowed me to track his growth and make changes to his diet or exercise routine as needed. I'm happy to say that after using the calculator for a few weeks, I was able to see that my puppy's weight was on track to reach a healthy adult weight. This project not only helped me monitor my puppy's growth but also allowed me to apply my programming skills to a real-life situation.
